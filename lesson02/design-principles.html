Principles of Design

Zachary Robker

Contrast - Best Buy

As seen in the website above, Best Buy has implemented a good use of contrast. One example of how they use contrast to their advantage is through color. The red outline where it says "Top Deals" uses the contrast of the red on blue to draw attention to this first. Then we can see they have used the same technique with the yellow on the "Shop Now" button and also for the descriptions under each image. All of this makes the page easy to read and also directs the user where to look and to what is most important.

White Space - Google

Google is probably a perfect example of a good use of white space. As seen above, they focus on what is most important on their website and don't shy away from leaving the rest blank and simple. The page has you first focus on the Google logo itself, then the search bar, leaving all other space white. I believe this is perfect for this website since its purpose is for its users to search for other topics. Therefore, by leaving blank white space it allows the user to focus on what they came to do and not get distracted by other images or unnecessary text.

Proximity - The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints

The Church of Jesus Christ's website does a very good job at using the principle of proximity to make their website more understandable to the user. On the website there can be found many articles, videos, and images. With each of these, they use proximity between the text descriptions and the images to show a connection. As seen above many go as far as having the text overlap onto the image. This gives the user a clear indication of which description goes with which image.
